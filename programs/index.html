Rates & Fees

Parent & Baby Lessons (6 M to 3 Years)

These are our parent and baby classes, which will require a parent in the pool along with the instructor.

Child Lessons (4 Years to 17 Years)

These lessons are suitable for school-age children and teenagers.

Adult Lessons

These mixed classes are suitable for both male and female swimmers and may have either a male or a female instructor.

Female-Only Sessions

If you prefer, these female-only sessions have only female swimmers, as well as female instructors.

Children – Private Sessions

These are one-to-one sessions for babies, toddlers, and children.

Adults – Private Sessions

These are our one-to-one sessions for adult swimmers.

Water Aerobics for Women

These are female-only classes taught by a female instructor three times a week: Fridays, 6:00 p.m. | Saturdays, 8:00 a.m. | Sundays, 5:15 p.m.

Water Aerobics for Men

These are male-only classes taught once a week on Sundays at 6:15 p.m.

Private Pool Hire

There is a one-off registration fee which includes a swim cap and free leisure swimming on Sundays. Classes can be taken once a week over 10 weeks or twice a week over 5 weeks. Furthermore, we offer a 10% family discount for every additional family member.

Rates vary according to client’s requirements

Level Charts

Our swimming levels are structured to help swimmers progress confidently, step by step — from babies to adults mastering advanced aquatic skills.

Level 1.1 – Seahorse

Introduction to Mum and Baby in Water.

Goal: Build comfort and connection with water.

Level 1.2 – Starfish

Introduction to submerging and water play.

Goal: Encourage trust and underwater comfort.

Level 1.3 – Sea Turtle

Developing confidence and simple water skills.

Goal: Improve balance and coordination in water.

Level 1.4 – Seal

Self-discipline and transition to independent swimming.

Goal: Move confidently without support.

Level 2 – Fish

Junior introduction to learn to swim.

Goal: Build independence and breathing control.

Level 3 – Penguin

Junior beginners working on basic strokes.

Goal: Gain stroke confidence and control.

Level 4 – Dolphin

Junior intermediate swimmers improving endurance.

Goal: Strengthen strokes and swim longer distances.

Level 5 – Whale

Junior advanced swimmers mastering techniques.

Goal: Achieve strength, stamina, and refined strokes.

Level 6 – Bronze Submarine

Senior introduction to strokes and water confidence.

Goal: Transition to structured stroke training.

Level 7 – Silver Submarine

Senior beginners developing endurance and form.

Goal: Swim efficiently across multiple strokes.

Level 8 – Gold Submarine

Senior intermediate — focus on advanced techniques and drills.

Goal: Prepare for advanced aquatic skills.

Level 9 – Diamond Submarine

Senior advanced swimmers refining all strokes.

Goal: Achieve high proficiency and fluid motion.

Level 10 – Emerald Snorkel

Senior introduction to snorkeling techniques.

Goal: Build comfort and control underwater.

Level 11 – Ruby Aquatic

Senior introduction to life saving and rescue techniques.

Goal: Learn safety, awareness, and rescue skills.
